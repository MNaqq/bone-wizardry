Stroke Syndromes

Which artery, which deficit, which side. The board question is always: "this patient's symptoms localize to ____."

?
Quick — where's the lesion?
▼

A 68-year-old woman presents with right arm and face weakness. Her leg is spared. She can't speak but can understand you. Where is the stroke?

If you said left MCA — good instinct. Face + arm without leg = MCA territory. Expressive aphasia (Broca's) confirms left hemisphere. The leg being spared is the key — that's ACA territory.

If you weren't sure, that's exactly why this page exists. Let's build the map.

The Big Five

Know These Cold

Each artery has a signature. Learn the signature, not the list.

1
MCA — The Big One
▼

The MCA supplies the lateral cortex — motor and sensory strips for face and arm, Broca's area, Wernicke's area, and the lenticulostriate arteriesSmall branches off the MCA that feed the basal ganglia and internal capsule. When these rupture in hypertension, you get a deep hemorrhagic stroke — not the same as MCA territory cortical stroke..

🔑 MCA signature: Contralateral face + arm weakness with leg spared. If the leg is weak too, think ACA involvement or internal capsule (lacunar).

Left MCA = face/arm weakness + aphasia (Broca's if frontal, Wernicke's if temporal, global if both).

Right MCA = face/arm weakness + hemispatial neglect (patient ignores the left side of the world).

FindingLocalizes To
Broca's aphasia (can understand, can't speak)Left frontal (inferior frontal gyrus)
Wernicke's aphasia (fluent but nonsense)Left temporal (superior temporal gyrus)
Hemispatial neglectRight parietal
Contralateral homonymous hemianopiaOptic radiation involvement
⚠ BOARD TRAP
A patient with global aphasia + right hemiplegia = left MCA occlusion. But if they ONLY have leg weakness, that's ACA — don't pick MCA just because it's the most common stroke.
🔑 Memory Hook — tap to reveal
MCA = "Makes the Contralateral Arm" weak. Face and arm live on the lateral strip. Leg hangs over the medial edge — that's ACA territory.
2
ACA — The Leg Artery
▼

The ACA supplies the medial surface of the frontal and parietal lobes. The motor and sensory homunculus for the leg hangs over the medial edge of the hemisphere — right in ACA territory.

🔑 ACA signature: Contralateral leg weakness and sensory loss, with arm and face spared. This is the opposite of MCA.

ACA strokes can also cause personality changes (medial frontal = behavioral regulation), urinary incontinence, and alien hand syndromeThe affected hand acts on its own — reaches for objects, interferes with the other hand. Caused by damage to the supplementary motor area or corpus callosum. Rare but highly tested because it's memorable..

FindingWhy
Contralateral leg weaknessLeg motor strip is on the medial surface
Personality change / apathyMedial frontal cortex (behavioral regulation)
Urinary incontinenceMicturition center in medial frontal lobe
Alien hand syndromeSupplementary motor area / corpus callosum
🔑 Memory Hook — tap to reveal
ACA = "A leg, C? Absolutely." Leg weakness with personality change = ACA until proven otherwise. If someone's peeing on themselves and acting weird and their leg doesn't work, it's not dementia — it's ACA stroke.
3
PCA — The Vision Artery
▼

The PCA supplies the occipital lobe (primary visual cortex) and the inferior temporal lobe. It also feeds the thalamusVia thalamoperforating and thalamogeniculate branches. Thalamic stroke from PCA occlusion causes contralateral sensory loss, sometimes with pain (Dejerine-Roussy syndrome — thalamic pain syndrome). via perforating branches.

🔑 PCA signature: Contralateral homonymous hemianopia with macular sparing. The patient is blind in one visual field but can still see straight ahead because the macula gets dual blood supply (MCA + PCA).

Left PCA can cause alexia without agraphia — the patient can write but can't read what they wrote. Wild, but real. Damage to the left visual cortex + splenium of the corpus callosum disconnects visual input from the language centers.

FindingWhy
Homonymous hemianopia (macular sparing)Occipital cortex — macula has dual supply
Alexia without agraphiaLeft PCA + splenium disconnection
Prosopagnosia (can't recognize faces)Bilateral fusiform gyrus (inferior temporal)
Thalamic pain syndromeThalamoperforating branches
⚠ BOARD TRAP
Homonymous hemianopia without macular sparing = MCA stroke (optic radiation, not visual cortex). With macular sparing = PCA. The macular sparing is the differentiator.
🔑 Memory Hook — tap to reveal
PCA = "Practically Can't see Anything." If the main complaint is vision loss (hemianopia), think posterior. If it's weakness, think anterior.
4
Basilar — The Deadly One
▼

The basilar artery feeds the pons, cerebellum, and midbrain. Basilar occlusion is a neurological emergency — if you lose the pons, you lose consciousness, breathing, and everything below.

⚠ Locked-in syndrome: Basilar artery thrombosis destroys the ventral pons. Patient is fully conscious but can only move their eyes (vertical gaze preserved because CN III nucleus is in the midbrain, above the lesion). Awake. Aware. Trapped.

Lateral pontine syndrome (AICA occlusion): ipsilateral facial paralysis, hearing loss, ataxia + contralateral pain/temperature loss in the body. The "crossed findings"Brainstem strokes produce CROSSED deficits: ipsilateral cranial nerve signs (CN nuclei are IN the brainstem) + contralateral motor/sensory (tracts CROSS at the brainstem level). This is the board's favorite brainstem trick. are the giveaway for any brainstem stroke.

SyndromeArteryKey Finding
Locked-in syndromeBasilar trunkQuadriplegia, anarthria, preserved vertical eye movement + consciousness
Lateral pontine (AICA)AICAIpsilateral facial paralysis + deafness + ataxia
Medial pontineParamedian branchesContralateral hemiparesis + ipsilateral CN VI palsy
Lateral medullary (Wallenberg)PICADysphagia, hoarseness, ipsilateral Horner's, contralateral pain/temp loss
⚠ BOARD TRAP
Locked-in ≠ coma. The patient is AWAKE. EEG shows normal wakefulness. They can hear everything you say. The board loves to ask: "What test confirms consciousness?" Answer: ask them to blink or look up.
🔑 Memory Hook — tap to reveal
Wallenberg = "lateral medullary = PICA." Dysphagia + hoarseness + Horner's + "I can't feel pain on one side of my body but the OTHER side of my face" = crossed findings = brainstem = Wallenberg. The board LOVES this one.
5
Lacunar — The Small Vessel Strokes
▼

Lacunar strokes are small infarcts in the deep brain structures — basal ganglia, thalamus, internal capsule, pons. Caused by lipohyalinosisThickening and hyaline deposition in the walls of small penetrating arteries. Caused by chronic hypertension. These tiny vessels can't handle the pressure and either occlude or rupture. Hypertension is the #1 risk factor for lacunar strokes. of small penetrating arteries. Hypertension is the #1 cause.

🔑 Lacunar signature: Pure syndromes — pure motor, pure sensory, or both. NO cortical signs (no aphasia, no neglect, no visual field cuts). If there's cortical involvement, it's not lacunar.
SyndromeLocationPresentation
Pure motor hemiparesisPosterior limb of internal capsuleFace + arm + leg weakness, NO sensory loss
Pure sensory strokeVPL nucleus of thalamusFace + arm + leg numbness, NO weakness
Ataxic hemiparesisPosterior limb / ponsWeakness + cerebellar ataxia on same side
Dysarthria-clumsy handBasis pontis / genu of internal capsuleSlurred speech + hand clumsiness

The internal capsule is the board's favorite lacunar target. Everything passes through it — motor tracts, sensory tracts, everything. A tiny lacunar infarct here causes complete contralateral hemiparesis (face + arm + leg, because ALL the motor fibers are bundled together at this point).

⚠ BOARD TRAP
Lacunar stroke with face + arm + leg weakness looks like MCA at first. The difference: no cortical signs. No aphasia. No neglect. No hemianopia. If the weakness is "too pure" — just motor, nothing else — think lacunar.
🔑 Memory Hook — tap to reveal
"Pure = Poor little vessels." Lacunar strokes are PURE — pure motor, pure sensory. The moment you see cortical signs (aphasia, neglect, visual field cuts), you've left lacunar territory and entered MCA/ACA/PCA land.
